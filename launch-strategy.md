## What Could Go Wrong (And How to Handle It)

### Scenario 1: Nobody Cares (0 stars, 0 engagement)

**Response:**
- Don't panic - most projects take 3-6 months to gain traction
- Keep building in public
- Focus on quality over quantity (write better content)
- Reach out personally to 50 developers 1-on-1

### Scenario 2: Negative Feedback ("This is stupid")

**Response:**
- Listen carefully - they might be right about specific issues
- Don't get defensive
- Thank them for feedback
- Ask: "What would you change to make this work?"
- Update whitepaper based on valid critiques

### Scenario 3: Someone Says "This Already Exists"

**Response:**
- Research the existing solution thoroughly
- If they're right, acknowledge it and pivot
- If they're wrong, explain the differences clearly
- Example: "ENS does naming, but not federated OAuth. UHP does both."

### Scenario 4: Professor Says It's Cryptographically Flawed

**Response:**
- This is actually GOOD - they took time to review!
- Ask for specifics
- Fix the flaw
- Thank them profusely
- Ask if they'd review v0.2

### Scenario 5: Overwhelming Response (1,000 stars in 1 day)

**Response:**
- Don't let hype go to your head
- Focus on converting stars to contributors
- Set up infrastructure (Discord mods, GitHub templates)
- Keep quality high (don't rush to ship broken code)
